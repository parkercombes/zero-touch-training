Design decisions reflected in this mock

  • Badge is the only training UI on the launchpad — the tile itself is unchanged and navigates to the real app as normal. There's no second tile, no separate training group.
  • Badge position: top-right corner, overlapping the tile edge with a white border to lift it visually. Works for both icon tiles and dynamic/KPI tiles.
  • Two badge styles shown — solid fill with level number (tiles 2, 3, 6) vs. SVG progress ring (tile 4). The ring style conveys percentage completion at a glance; the number conveys current level. Both carry the same data — pick one to standardize.
  • Popover on badge tap (not tile tap) — tapping the tile still opens the SAP app. Tapping the badge opens the training popover. This preserves the normal Fiori navigation contract.
  • Popover contains level progress track + Resume + Open App — the "Open App" secondary button lets a user who's already trained go straight into the real transaction.
  • No badge = no training scenario linked to this app — avoids visual noise on tiles that don't have training content yet. Admins can link scenarios via the manager dashboard.
  • Manager banner at top — visible to supervisor roles only. Shows team-level completion stats and quick links to assign training or view progress.
  • Minimum tap target: badge is 26px + 10px negative margin = 46px effective tap area. Acceptable for gloved warehouse use; increase badge to 32px for full 52px compliance.
